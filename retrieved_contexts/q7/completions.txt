

Yes, there are ways to lower your risk of developing Alzheimer's disease. Research has shown that lifestyle factors such as regular exercise, a heart-healthy diet, reducing stress, and quitting smoking can help promote healthy brain aging and reduce the risk of Alzheimer's disease. Additionally, having fewer years of education and having certain conditions such as diabetes and high blood pressure can increase your risk of developing Alzheimer's disease. It is important to be aware of these risk factors and take steps to reduce them.
